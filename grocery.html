For those of you considering moving to a zero-waste lifestyle, the biggest consideration you might have in London is... how do I do my food shopping?? With a proliferation of grocery stores in London focusing on convenience rather than our planet, making the move can seem daunting.

Never fear! We've compiled a list of our favorite shops for bulk shopping! Now gather up your jars, sacks, bottles, and reusable shopping totes and hit the road!

This is not the most extensive zero waste shopping guide available, nor the one with the most information (for that I like the eco boost post on the subject), but we think it could be a good start.

Most Common

Planet Organic

Planet Organic has become our go-to all-purpose grocery shop, especially as we are lucky enough to have one in the neighborhood.

  • Best for: Dry goods, like rice, porridge, nuts, and lentils. Also can get baking chocolate! We like that we can weigh our goods and containers seamlessly to mke the checkout process efficient.
  • Room for improvement: While the goods you get are packaging- free, which we applaud, we have noticed that they refill their bins with good that often come to them in plastic. We want to minimize all packaging but especially plastic.

Most International

Whole Foods

While we don't normally advocate Amazon as a zero-waste hero, Whole Foods was into bulk shopping before it was cool. The mega-store in South Ken is the place to go to be filled with child-like wonder, and also for bulk flours, beans, and grind-your-own peanut butter.

  • Best for: Pasta, beans, grains, and peanut butter.
  • Room for improvement: We prefer to shop local if we can, and Whole Foods has burned us in the past when it comes to bringing our own containers, leading to stress at the checkout line.

Best Shop for Spices

Earth Natural Foods

This Kentish Town natural foods store has a delightful array of dry goods sold by weight, including a selection of beans and !!! wholemeal pasta. What I love most about it though is their spice wall and the aura of good feeling that permeates the shop. The checkout process can be a bit long but it's a great place to visit.

  • Best for: Spices, pasta, more unusual grains.
  • Room for imporovement: Checkout efficiency.

Most Plastic-Free

Hetu

This utopian vegan plastic-free bulk shop is dreamy. Tiny, and located South of the River in Clapham Junction, they have a rotating stock, but generally a good selection of staples and treats. In addition to dry goods and spices, they also carry maple syrup and plastic-free accessories, like bamboo compostable toothbrushes. Chat with the owner if she's around, she is full of zero waste tips and knows her way around the London plastic-free scene.

  • Best for: surprising yourself (I've found bulgar wheat, soybeans, pasta and other things) - the stock changes so it's best to go without a firm list in mind. You can also use jars from their stock if you didn't bring enough.

  • Room for improvement: I'll let you know if I can think of anything.

Most Anticipated

Bulk Market

Starting as a pop-up shop on Hackney, this zero waste full-service grocery store promises to have it all, including beer, wine, and a community composting scheme. We visited while it was still a pop-up shop and it was delightful... now if it would just open!

  • Best for: Dry good staples and a zero waste community.
  • Room for Improvement: We'll see.

Honorable Mentions

Where to Buy Specialty Goods

For us, buying things like coffee and wine are also important and we were delighted to find some zero-waste gems that suit our purposes.

  • Monmouth Coffee: With a few locations, this top-notch coffee roaster is happy to fill a bag for you with freshly roasted coffee beans (wholebean or ground). They also sell ceramic V60 pour-over cones if you want to go minimalist for coffee prep.
