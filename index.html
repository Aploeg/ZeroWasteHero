What is Zero-Waste?

The Zero-Waste movement seeks to reduce waste in all facets of life, ranging from literal physical waste (e.g. rubbish) to wasted time and wasted money. This philosophy can be applied to individuals, families, and businesses and the most well-known guidebook is Bea Johnson's Zero-Waste Home. The concept is fairly well-known, but can also seem overwhelming for some, especially for busy people living in a city like London.

We are here to help. The purpose of this blog is to help you take the steps that make sense for you to move towards reducing waste in your own life.

Getting Started

You can think of this lifestyle change as a series of complimentary steps. Say, for instance, that you don't have a good picture in what your everyday waste looks like. Try for just one day to save any waste and look at it at the end of the day. This means that if you buy a coffee for takeaway, save the cup. If you use pre-chopped veg from M&S, save the container. At the end of the day, take a look at what has accumulated. What seems unecessary for you? A good first step could be, for instance, using a reusable mug, or carrying a resuable water bottle. For someone else, it might mean visiting a produce shop once a week and spending an hour on the weekend chopping vegetables to use the rest of the week.

The transition to zero-waste does not need to happen all at once. Often just taking some time to think about your everyday decisions may inspire you to make changes that benefit both the planet and you wallet.

How to Use This Site

We have broken this site into pages meant to make a shift towards zero-waste easier in certain areas of your life. The Groceries page has a list of some places to shop for food in London with less packaging. The Produce page has some information on farm markets or other ideas for buying packaging-free fruits and veg. Cleaning includes some DIY ideas for household chores while Self-Care has information on what we've learned about substitution for common chemical-and-packaging laden toiletries.
